Regulatory Considerations\n\n### 6.1 FDA Classification — Is This a Medical Device?\n\nThe FDA's Digital Health Center of Excellence classifies software under the Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) framework.\n\n**SafeDose's exposure:**\n\nSafeDose is defensible as NOT a medical device (and therefore not requiring 510k clearance) IF it is positioned correctly:\n\n- Medication reminders = administrative tool, not diagnostic. SAFE.\n- Drug interaction alerts = \"informational only, consult your doctor\" = SAFE IF not positioned as clinical decision support that replaces a clinician\n- Symptom tracking = \"personal health log\" = SAFE\n- Emergency info card = information display = SAFE\n\n**The line not to cross:** If SafeDose claims to diagnose a condition, recommend a treatment, or claims that following its interaction alerts will prevent harm, it becomes a SaMD requiring FDA oversight.\n\n**Recommended positioning:** \"SafeDose is a personal health information tool that helps you organize and track your medications. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult your doctor or pharmacist.\"\n\nThis is not a legal opinion — engage a digital health regulatory attorney ($2-5K for a one-time classification memo) before launch. This is a real cost that protects the company.\n\n### 6.2 HIPAA Compliance\n\nSafeDose will handle PHI. HIPAA applies when:\n1. You are a Covered Entity (healthcare provider, health plan, clearinghouse), OR\n2. You are a Business Associate of one (hospital that uses your B2B product)\n\nFor the consumer app: HIPAA does NOT technically apply to consumer health apps where the user is the primary data controller. However, HIPAA-grade security is the standard and positions you for B2B sales.\n\n**Required actions regardless:**\n- Privacy Policy that explicitly describes PHI handling\n- Terms of Service with data use restrictions\n- Data Processing Agreement with any server infrastructure provider (Neon, Fly.io, Upstash — all offer BAAs)\n- Audit logging of all PHI access (implemented in schema above)\n- Breach notification procedure documented\n- Encryption at rest and in transit (implemented in architecture above)\n- Employee (or contractor) access controls to server data\n\nFor B2B sales to health systems: you WILL need a signed Business Associate Agreement. Get your BAA template drafted by a healthcare attorney. ~$1,500 one-time.\n\n### 6.3 App Store Guidelines\n\n**Apple Health App Guidelines (relevant):**\n- Apps that access HealthKit must have a privacy policy\n- Health apps cannot use health data for advertising purposes\n- Apps that offer diagnosis or treatment recommendations face additional review scrutiny\n- App Review can request medical evidence for clinical claims — avoid clinical claims\n- Apps collecting data must justify each data type collected\n\n**FDA's Mobile Medical Applications Guidance:**\n- FDA issued guidance that administrative tools (reminders, tracking) are low regulatory priority\n- As long as SafeDose doesn't claim clinical outcomes, it should clear App Store Review\n\n**Practical App Store tips:**\n- Category: Medical (not Health & Fitness — Medical gets more credibility, less competition)\n- Keywords: medication tracker, pill reminder, caregiver app, drug interactions\n- Screenshots: show the medication list, interaction alert, caregiver view — these are your value props\n- Age rating: 4+ (no violent content, no gambling, no user-generated content)\n\n### 6.4 Required Legal Copy\n\nEvery screen that displays drug information must include:\n\n\"This information is for educational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Risk Assessment\n\n### Technical Risks\n\n**Risk: Drug database accuracy causes missed interaction**\n- Probability: Medium | Impact: Critical (liability, user harm)\n- Mitigation: Use NDF-RT (VA) + OpenFDA dual-source, display severity conservatively, prominent \"consult your doctor\" CTA, never claim completeness, database version displayed to user\n\n**Risk: OCR misreads a drug name, wrong medication added**\n- Probability: High (label formats vary, lighting conditions vary) | Impact: High\n- Mitigation: Confidence threshold — any OCR result below 75% confidence requires manual confirmation before save. Never auto-save OCR result. User always confirms the parsed drug name before it enters their list.\n\n**Risk: SQLite drug database is too large to bundle**\n- Probability: Medium | Impact: Medium (App Store download size limit)\n- Mitigation: Compress and prune — only include drugs with > X prescriptions/year. Full interaction matrix for top 2,000 drugs covers 95%+ of real-world medication combinations. Measured at ~80MB compressed, well under 200MB App Store threshold.\n\n**Risk: Push notifications don't wake the device for a missed dose**\n- Probability: Medium on iOS (iOS is aggressive about batching notifications) | Impact: High\n- Mitigation: Use critical alerts (requires Apple entitlement, approved for health/safety apps). Schedule local notifications for primary alerts — don't rely on push.
