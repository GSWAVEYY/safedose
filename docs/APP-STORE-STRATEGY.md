## App Store Optimization (ASO)

### App Name & Subtitle
- **Name:** SafeDose (30 char limit — 8 chars)
- **Subtitle:** "Medication & Interaction Checker" (33 chars — slightly over. Alternative: "Med Tracker & Interaction Check" at 31 chars — use this if Apple enforces strictly. Preferred: "Medication & Interaction Checker" — test submission.)
- **Why:** "Medication" is the #1 search term. "Interaction Checker" is the differentiator. Together they describe what SafeDose does.

### Keywords Field (100 characters, comma-separated)
```
medication tracker,drug interaction,pill reminder,caregiver,medicine,health,elderly,prescription,safe
```

**Keyword strategy:**
- "medication tracker" — highest intent keyword. Users searching this want exactly what SafeDose does.
- "drug interaction" — differentiator. Most "pill reminder" apps don't do this. SafeDose does.
- "pill reminder" — high volume, moderate competition. Captures the reminder-only audience who'll discover interactions as a bonus.
- "caregiver" — lower volume but extremely high intent. Caregivers searching for apps are ready to download.
- "medicine" — international users and less tech-savvy users search "medicine" not "medication"
- "health" — broad, high competition. Worth including for long-tail match potential.
- "elderly" — low volume but zero competition. Adult children search "elderly medication app."
- "prescription" — captures users managing prescription medications specifically.
- "safe" — brand reinforcement + natural search term ("safe medication app")

## Apple Health App Review Preparation

Health apps receive additional scrutiny during Apple review. Prepare for these potential questions:

### "Does this app diagnose medical conditions?"
**Answer:** No. SafeDose tracks medications and checks for known interactions using public FDA databases (RxNorm, NDF-RT). It does not diagnose, recommend treatment, or suggest dosage changes. All decisions are made by the user in consultation with their healthcare provider.

### "What are your sources for drug interaction data?"
**Answer:** RxNorm (National Library of Medicine / NIH) and NDF-RT (Veterans Administration / NLM). Both are public domain, freely available FDA-referenced databases.

### "How do you handle user health data?"
**Answer:** All medication data is stored on-device only (free tier) or encrypted with AES-256-GCM before server sync (premium tier). The server never sees plaintext medication data. Zero-knowledge architecture. See our privacy policy at [URL].

### "Does the app include appropriate disclaimers?"
**Answer:** Yes. The disclaimer "SafeDose is not a substitute for professional medical advice" appears in: onboarding, settings, every interaction alert, the App Store description, and the privacy policy.

**Review notes to include with submission:**
> SafeDose is a medication management tool that uses public FDA databases (RxNorm, NDF-RT) for interaction checking. It does not diagnose, treat, or prescribe. All interaction alerts include a disclaimer directing users to consult their pharmacist. All medication data stays on-device (free tier) or is encrypted before any sync (premium tier). Demo account available at [login/password] if needed for review.

## Review Management

### Responding to Reviews

**Health app reviews require extra care.** Users may share personal health information in reviews. Responses must be empathetic and never dismissive.

**Positive reviews (4-5 stars):**
> "Thank you — we're glad SafeDose is helping with your [medication management / caregiving]. Your privacy matters to us, and we'll keep improving."

**Bug reports (1-3 stars):**
> "We're sorry about this. Medication management tools need to work reliably, and we take this seriously. We've identified the issue and a fix is coming in [version]. If you need help now, please email support@vraxondigital.com."

**Interaction accuracy concerns:**
> "Thank you for flagging this. SafeDose uses FDA databases (RxNorm, NDF-RT) which are the most comprehensive public source available, but no database catches every interaction. We always recommend confirming with your pharmacist. We'll investigate this specific combination."

**Never say in a review response:**
- "SafeDose is accurate" (absolute claims about medical data)
- "You don't need to worry about interactions" (dismissing health concerns)
- "This interaction isn't dangerous" (medical advice)

### Review Prompt Timing
- Trigger SKStoreReviewController after: 7+ days AND 10+ reminder confirmations AND 0 crashes
- Never prompt immediately after an interaction alert (stressful moment)
- Never prompt during medication entry (interrupts workflow)
- Best moment: after viewing a weekly adherence summary showing good adherence (positive emotional state)

## Update Cadence

- **Monthly updates** for the first 6 months
- **Drug database updates** whenever FDA publishes new RxNorm/NDF-RT data (quarterly minimum)
- **"What's New" format:** Lead with safety-relevant changes. "Updated drug interaction database with [X] new interaction pairs. Fixed a bug where [specific fix]. Added [user-requested feature]."
- **Version numbering:** Semantic versioning. Drug database updates = minor version bump (1.1 → 1.2). Feature additions = minor. Breaking changes = major.
