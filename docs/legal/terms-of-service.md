SafeDose provides drug interaction information sourced from public databases maintained by the National Institutes of Health (RxNorm) and the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (NDF-RT). This information is provided for **reference and informational purposes only**.

**SafeDose's drug interaction database:**
- May not include every known drug interaction
- May not reflect the most recently discovered interactions
- Does not account for your individual health conditions, allergies, kidney/liver function, or other factors that affect how drugs interact in your body
- Does not account for supplements, herbal products, or over-the-counter medications you may be taking
- Is not a replacement for pharmacist review of your complete medication profile

**What you should do with interaction information from SafeDose:** Bring it to your pharmacist or doctor. Ask them whether the flagged interaction is clinically significant for you specifically.

**What you should not do:** Make medication changes based solely on information from SafeDose without consulting a healthcare professional.

### 3b. Missed Interactions

SafeDose may not flag an interaction that exists. Database gaps are real. If you are concerned about any combination of medications, consult your pharmacist directly — they have access to more complete interaction databases and your full medical history.

SafeDose is not responsible for missed doses that result from a reminder failure. Do not rely solely on SafeDose reminders for time-critical medications.
